The core insight: LFM2.5-230M can extract structured data correctly — it just gets trapped in bad output patterns. Adding Gaussian noise (σ=0.01, 0.5% relative perturbation) to the embedding layer during generation lets it escape these "bad basins." Selecting the best of K rollouts by NLL then picks the correct completion. Inspired by PTRM (arXiv:2605.19943).

Sigma Sweep: How Much Noise?

Best-of-10 accuracy at different noise magnitudes. Razor-thin operating range: σ=0.005–0.015 works, cliff at 0.02.

⚠️
Why so narrow? PTRM uses σ=0.2–1.0 because their recursive architecture processes noise once per step. In autoregressive generation, noise accumulates at every token. The operating range is ~20× narrower than PTRM's. Relative perturbation must stay under 1% of the residual stream norm.

Head-to-Head: Does Noise Injection Point Matter?

All embedding-noise conditions produce identical results at safe σ. The model's own architecture propagates perturbation naturally.

ConditionAccuracyField RecallK
No noise25.0%26.7%10
Uniform (all layers)80.0%94.0%10
Hub only (L0)80.0%94.0%10
Random layer (L7)80.0%94.0%10
Early phase (L0-L5)80.0%94.0%10
Atlas-scaled (σ∝importance)0.0%1.7%10
🔬
Atlas-scaled fails because the importance-weighted σ pushes L0's noise to ~0.115 — well past the 0.02 cliff. The atlas is correct about which layers matter, but for safe noise levels, the injection point is irrelevant. The atlas becomes critical only when pushing into dangerous σ territory (targeting safe weak layers with higher noise).

What This Enables

🚀 Free inference-time capability boost

Any deployment of LFM2.5-230M for structured extraction can use 5 noisy rollouts + NLL selection. ~77% accuracy at 5x compute. No retraining, no data.

🧪 Alternative to SFT for tiny models

Phase 9 showed 300 examples = catastrophic overfitting at 230M. Noise-based exploration avoids this entirely — you don't need training data, you need inference diversity.

📊 PTRM generalizes to autoregressive models

PTRM claimed their technique works on recursive models. We've shown it works on autoregressive models too — with 20× narrower σ range due to per-step amplification.

🎯 Trained Q-head is the next unlock

Oracle selection gets 80%, NLL-based gets 57.5%. A lightweight MLP on rollout embeddings could close the 22.5pp gap. ~100 training examples should suffice.

Methodology

Model: LiquidAI/LFM2.5-230M (230M params, 14 hybrid conv+attn layers, 450MB bf16)

Hardware: aero (RTX 2070 Super 8GB)

Noise method: Gaussian noise added to model.model.embed_tokens output via forward hook. Seed-controlled for reproducibility.

Eval prompts: 50 structured extraction prompts (name/age/city, product/price, etc.) with ground truth JSON.

Scoring: Field-level recall (substring match of expected values in generated text). Exact match = all fields found.

Judge: mimo-v2.5 via OpenCode Go API. Pointwise scoring on 6 dimensions (1-5). Pairwise comparison (A vs B).

Total compute: ~55 minutes for 6 experiments + ~15 minutes for judge evaluation.

Paper Reference

Probabilistic Tiny Recursive Model (PTRM) — arXiv:2605.19943

Injects Gaussian noise during inference to escape "bad basins" in recursive models. Uses K parallel rollouts + Q-head selection. Achieves 91.2% on PPBench with 7M params.

Our work generalizes this to autoregressive models and shows the technique works with embedding-level noise, not just latent-state noise.
